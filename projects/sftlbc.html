Brief

S.F. True Light Baptist Church Website Redesign

We spearheaded an end-to-end design process for the S.F. True Light Baptist Church website to increase engagement and visibility for users by 63%.

Background

The ten-year-old website of S.F. True Light Baptist Church requires a redesign due to its outdated visual style and lack of interactive features. The current layout is cluttered and lacks modern design elements, making it difficult for users, especially new visitors, to navigate and find relevant information. Improving the website's aesthetics and functionality will enhance user engagement, better communicate church activities, and attract new members.

Stakeholders

The pastor of S.F. True Light Baptist Church approached us to redesign their website in order to attract new members and enhance engagement within the church community. I am working with five key stakeholders: the Pastor, an Elder, the Deacon Board, another UX Designer, and a Developer. Each stakeholder group has different priorities, impacts, and associated risks that we need to consider throughout the redesign process.

challenge

How might we encourage engagement and increase conversions on the website?

The ten-year-old website of S.F. True Light Baptist Church needs a redesign to improve navigation and update its visual style, enhancing user engagement and effectively communicating church activities to attract new members.

Goal

Redesign the S.F. True Light Baptist Church website to improve user navigation, update visual aesthetics, and enhance engagement to attract and retain members.

My Contribution

I led the redesign of our organization's website, boosting user engagement by 63% through strategic SEO, keyword optimization, and tailored content. By conducting thorough research and collaborating with engineers and designers using tools like Figma and FigJam, I ensured the final design was innovative, user-friendly, and aligned with our strategic goals.

Understand

Observation

We visited the church on a service day to observe the entire environment, people's behaviors, and the worship service. The photos show various aspects of church life, from community activities to the worship setting, helping us understand the dynamics and needs of the congregation.

Questionnaire

To understand our audience, we conducted a questionnaire survey, as shown in the image. The survey results indicate that while 66% of respondents have visited the S.F. True Light Baptist Church website, many are not regular visitors, suggesting room for improvement in engagement. The main discovery channel is through friends' referrals, highlighting the importance of personal connections. The preferred methods of accessing church information are WeChat and printed weekly announcements, indicating a need for both digital and traditional communication channels. This suggests the target audience values community ties and a mix of modern and traditional information sources.

Target Audience

After in-depth communication with the church pastor, elders, and deacon board, and using the church's demographics data, we refined our target audience. Given the church's established history, it is not surprising that the congregation tends to be older.

Recognizing that the 23-56 age group forms the website's most significant cohort, and noting the 4514 visitors since the website's launch in 2017, we conducted effective user research to segment the target audience into three categories:

  1. Primary User: New Members
    • Recently joined individuals seeking integration into the church community.
  2. Potential New Members (Online Visitors)
    • Individuals exploring the church website and considering joining, primarily aged 23-56.
  3. Established Members
    • Long-term, active members needing updated information and community engagement tools, predominantly aged 41+.

User Interview & Synthesis

To better understand users' perspectives on the website, we conducted interviews with 16 users from our three target audience categories: new members, established members, and potential new members.

New Members (6 People)

  • Informative and Up-to-date Content: New members want the site to be informative and regularly updated.
  • Event Photos and Information: 75% want to see photos of past events and get more event information.
  • Church Personnel: 80% are interested in knowing the composition of church personnel.
  • Weekly Sundays: 55% wish it were easier to watch weekly Sunday services.

Established Members (5 People)

  • Interaction with Members: Established members hope to have more interaction with other members.
  • Mobile Compatibility: 85% want the website to have a mobile-friendly page.

Potential New Members (5 People)

  • Font Readability: 90% think the Chinese fonts on the original church website are too crowded.
  • Terminology Understanding: Most do not know church terminology.
  • Effective Page Area: All think that the effective page area of the website is too small.

Competitive Analysis

In conclusion, my competitive analysis of AllTrails, BreezoMeter, and Atmotube has provided valuable insights into their strengths, such as extensive trail databases, advanced air quality monitoring technologies, and user-friendly interfaces. However, it also highlights notable weaknesses like restricted offline map access, data accuracy issues, and infrequent data visualizations. Each competitor has distinct opportunities and faces unique threats in the evolving landscape shaped by post-pandemic behavior and technological advancements. This analysis serves as a strategic guide for identifying areas of improvement and innovation in my project, ensuring I deliver a product that effectively meets the needs of outdoor enthusiasts with enhanced features and capabilities.

Interview

For better understanding my users, I conducted interviews with 3 target audiences and 2 experts, revealing a strong demand for an app that combines trail information with real-time environmental data. This app, aimed at outdoor enthusiasts, would provide essential details on air quality and wildfire conditions to ensure safety and enhance the planning of outdoor activities. Users highlighted the need for predictive alerts, trail amenities information, and an engaging rewards system. Experts advised focusing on core functionalities that prioritize outdoor safety and suggested simplifying features to avoid overwhelming users. They also pointed out the importance of technical feasibility and strategic user targeting. To refine these insights and ensure the development meets user expectations while addressing technical and strategic challenges, I used an affinity diagram to help me extract insights, aiming for a well-rounded, user-centric solution. In case I couldn't get the sweet spot accurately, the affinity diagram served as a crucial tool to distill insights further.

Insights & Takeaways

  • Wildfires significantly impact both the physical health and emotional well-being of individuals, causing fear and frustration due to unpredictability.
  • There is a critical need for quick, trustworthy information regarding the direction and safety of areas affected by wildfire smoke.
  • Users desire predictive insights into air quality and wildfire trends to effectively plan their activities.
  • Accuracy in real-time air quality and wildfire information is essential to avoid misinformation, which is a major source of user dissatisfaction.
  • Tailored information is necessary for users with specific health conditions, such as allergies, requiring more detailed and relevant data.
  • The goal is to develop a product that provides accurate, timely updates on air quality and wildfires, catering to the diverse needs of users, enhancing their safety, and aiding in informed decision-making.

    Prototype

    Information Architecture

    The information architecture for Trail Angel was meticulously designed from the ground up, inspired by insights gathered during the MVP development phase. It represents a well-thought-out blueprint of the app’s structure, detailing the organization and hierarchy of pages such as the home page, search filter, trail list, and more. This architecture is critical in establishing a logical flow for the application, guiding users naturally from one function to the next, ensuring that the core features are accessible and that the user journey is coherent and intuitive.

    Wireframe

    Moving from the abstract structure of information architecture to tangible interfaces, the wireframe creation process was informed by this groundwork. The wireframes translate the information architecture into detailed layouts for each page, considering the user interactions and visual elements. This step is where the theoretical framework of the app’s navigation is brought to life, allowing for the visualization of the user interface. It sets the stage for the actual design and development work, highlighting how users will engage with the app and where key features will be placed for optimal user experience.

    Visual Design

    Design System

    The name "Trail Angel" was conceived with a nod to the rich tradition of American hiking culture, where the term "trail angel" is affectionately used to describe someone who provides support to hikers. Embracing this spirit, I defined the brand personality of Trail Angel with five key adjectives: supportive, reliable, intuitive, adventurous, and empowering. These traits guided the creation of the Trail Angel logo and mood board, encapsulating the essence of the brand in visual form. The brand identity, rich with cultural resonance and personality, then served as the cornerstone for developing the app's design system, ensuring that every element from typography to color palettes aligns with the core values and character of Trail Angel.
