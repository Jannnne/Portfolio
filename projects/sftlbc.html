Design Process

Background

True Light Baptist Church, established in 2002, has about 90 members. The existing website for True Light Baptist Church was built in 2017. The old visual style of the website is not conducive to propaganda work and has almost no effect on communication and interaction within the church community. Especially during the epidemic, the old website could not meet the new needs of the church's development. Our stakeholders want the church website to become the primary carrier of information transmission, enhance the interaction between the new and old church members, and attract new visitors to join True Light Baptist Church. Therefore, the main redesign goals of the church website are to promote church information, recruit new members, and interact with the church.

Challenge

How might we encourage engagement and increase conversions on the website?

Solutions

  • timely information

    We provide call-to-action sections to access updated information quickly.

  • clarification

    It enables the user to understand the faith terms through explanations.

  • Efficiency

    We improve information architecture to make user and website interaction more effective.

Church Personaliy Survey

In order to better define the church branding style, we conducted a characteristic survey. From user perspective, the visual style including these elements: Up-to-date, Clear, informative, connected,spiritual, enlightening

Target Audience

After in-depth communication with stakeholders, according to the requirements and goals of stakeholders, the target users of the website are potential new members, new members who have joined, and old members. Using the church's demographics, we narrow down our target audience. Considering the established time of the church, it's no surprise that the followers tend to be older. 23-40 years old is 27%, 41-56 is 52%, and 57+ years old is 15%. Regarding gender, men and women are equally represented in the church.
So we determined that 23-56-year-olds are the website's most significant cohort. Since the old website launched in 2017, there have been 4514 visitors. To conduct effective user research, we separated the target audience into three types.

  • Primary user: New member
  • People have the potential to be a new member (online visitors)
  • Old members

According to the survey, we learned that a friend's reference and website are the best ways to join the community.

Observation

During California's nine-month wildfire season, people never know if they'll be in a polluted area from a wildfire at any time.

User Interview & Synthesis

To better understand user' perspectives of using the website, we conducted interviews with 3 type of 16 users.

We asked some questions and learned lots of insights as follows:

    New Member - 6 people
  • New members want the site to be informative and up-to-date.
  • 75% want to see photos of past events and get more event information.
  • 80% are more concerned about the composition of church personnel.
  • 55% wish it were easier to watch weekly Sundays.
    • Old members - 5 people
  • Old members hope to have more interaction with other members.
  • 85% want the website to have a mobile page.
    • Those who are not a member of the church - 5 people
  • 90% think the Chinese fonts on the original church website are too crowded.
  • Most don't know church terminology.
  • All think that the effective page area of the website is too small.

Affinity Diagram

We took the responses through an affinity diagram to find common themes amongst the responders.

Insights

Insight#1

Users expect the website to have enough information and continuous updates of the information.

Sub-insight#1

Users want to see photos and reviews of past events, and learn more event information.

Insight#2

Users expect the website to provide (proprietary term explanation, clarification) question education and faith relate.

Insight#3

Users hope to have more interaction with other fellows through the church.

With all the research and insights in mind, we moved into the next phase of actually building the website. Starting with some rough sketches on paper, we drew up some different concepts on the whiteboard, sought feedback from teammates and then brought the final ideas to life in Figma.

Information Architecture

To understand better how users associate and group information on this website, we invited target users to do cart sorting for new and existing websites. According to the responses, we drew the website’s site map and combined all the low-fi wireframe screens onto one board so that we were able to see and adjust user flows and navigation quickly.

Phase 5 - Deliver

Hand Off to Developers

After everything is ready, the files are about to be handed off to the developers. The design handoff is a point in the product development process where developers implement the finished design. We provided all the hand-off materials and answered any questions developers had about the design or functionality. The all necessary assets include both an interactive prototype and a Figma file with possible status of the screens, detailed descriptions, and design style guide.

What I Learned

Pros: Teamwork

Teamwork is essential in the world of UX design. Collaborating closely with developers has taught me the value of adopting a developer's mindset and considering the broader perspective. In my designs, I don't just focus on interface elements, user experience, and visuals; I also ensure that the product's functionality is well-addressed, translating my solutions into digital reality effectively.
Effective communication is the linchpin of successful teamwork and a seamless design handoff process. It's crucial to communicate proactively and frequently, using the most appropriate channels and methods to ensure a productive and harmonious collaboration.

Cons: Time Management

In the upcoming design cycle, I aim to enhance my time management and project management abilities. To achieve this, I intend to establish both short-term and long-term goals. I will also adopt a strategy of breaking down substantial project milestones into smaller, more manageable tasks, ensuring that each of these smaller milestones is attainable within its respective deadline. This approach will help me optimize my time and project management skills for greater efficiency and productivity.
