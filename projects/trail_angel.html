Overview

Trail Angel is a mobile app that assists active people to plan adventures outdoors without worrying about air pollution due to wildfires. The app includes a connection to a portable air tracker to monitor conditions in real-time. Trail Angel also provides alternative route planning should an area become unsafe, so outdoor activities can still be enjoyed safely.

Solution

On-time Information

Individuals can employ the portable air quality monitor to instantaneously visualize and continuously monitor real-time air quality.

Make a Trail Plan

Users can access a sophisticated platform that not only facilitates the creation of personalized outdoor trail plans but also provides real-time air quality index data. This empowers users to enjoy a pristine outdoor experience, ensuring they breathe in clean, fresh air throughout their journey.

Detour A Trail

Users can enhance awarenesses of real-time outdoor local air pollution alerts, enabling them to promptly assess the safety of their current location with respect to air quality.

Change A Trail Plan

Users can access real-time air pollution forecasts, allowing them to adjust their travel plans proactively prior to reaching their destination.

MVPs

Trail plan

The trail planning system we offer comes with some key features that make it a valuable resource for outdoor enthusiasts. By integrating an air quality index map, a wildfire history map, and prescribed fire sources, we empower users to make well-informed decisions when selecting their outdoor activities. This innovative approach brings several benefits to the table, primarily targeting sensitive groups of people. With this system, users can proactively steer clear of potentially unsafe trails, enhancing their outdoor experiences and safety. The technical features at the core of this solution include the AQI map, fire source data, air pollution information, and digital administration tools, all working together to provide a comprehensive and user-friendly trail planning experience for those who prioritize their health and well-being during outdoor adventures.


Wildfire Prediction

The app's primary objective is to leverage machine learning to provide valuable insights into California's wildfire history. By doing so, it empowers users to navigate and make informed decisions during wildfire seasons. Through this innovative technology, the app assists people in filtering wildfire-prone areas and identifying safe trails, all with the aim of ensuring their safety.
The benefits of this app are evident in its ability to guide individuals in creating trail plans that take into account potential fire and smoke risks. This critical functionality is designed to help users make well-informed choices, keeping them safe during their forest excursions.
The app's simple target audience comprises individuals who frequently spend extended periods of time in forested areas. It caters to the needs of hikers, campers, and outdoor enthusiasts, ensuring they can enjoy nature while staying aware of potential wildfire risks.
To make this all possible, the app utilizes technical features like the Wildfire history API and a wildfire filter, which work together to provide users with up-to-date and accurate information about wildfire history and the safety of specific areas. Through this combination of technology and data, the app offers a valuable resource for those seeking to enjoy the beauty of California's natural landscapes while minimizing the risks associated with wildfires.


AQI Alert

The air tracker, a remarkable application of the Internet of Things, plays a crucial role in providing real-time air pollution alerts to individuals. This innovative technology offers a multitude of benefits, with perhaps the most significant being the ability for people to promptly evacuate areas plagued by air pollution. This solution is particularly tailored to cater to a specific audience - the sensitive group of outdoor enthusiasts who depend on clean and safe air for their activities. Under the hood, the air tracker leverages technical features such as the Internet of Things, beacon technology, and Bluetooth connectivity to ensure that users are constantly informed and protected against harmful air quality conditions.

User Story

Wyatt is a product manager. He lives with his wife and six-year-old son Everett, and daughter Ivan. Every Saturday, Everett has soccer practice in the afternoon. Because the Wyatts live near Kikare Woods, sometimes the smoke from the Kikare wildfires can be carried by the wind to their neighbors. Soccer coaches have to cancel soccer training whenever wildfire smoke hits. The Wyatts had to stay home and wait for the smoke to pass. In any case, they can still smell a little smoke even when they stay at home. On Saturday, the football coach called Wyatt to tell him that the smoke had canceled football again. He suggested Wyatt download the app Trail Angel.
Wyatt installed Trail Angel on his phone. Wyatt creates an account on Trail Angel. On its home page has a button for creating a new trail plan. Wyatt thought it would be a good idea to find a place with good air quality for a hike so they wouldn't have to stay home and suffer from the smog. By using Trail Angel, Wyatt makes a trail plan immediately. Then they head out to the West Shore and Bass Cove Trail.

User Story

Harper and his buddies ride as a team. They do gravel cycling every weekend. Every time, when cyclists pass through the air pollution belt, they have to pass the polluted path or ride back. He and his friends prefer to leave high-pollution areas, so they use the Trail Angel app. The portable air tracker that works with Trail Angel alerts them when the AQI exceeds moderate levels.
Harper and his riding group started with the EI Corte de Madera Outer Loop pre-planned route, but within a few miles, the trackers began to ring. Harper stopped to check the call from TrailAngel. He got an alert about unsafe air quality which provided a detour with better air. He sent a Beacon text and rounded up his group. They detoured together to Tafoni Loop and then rode back along the EI Corte de Madera Outer Loop, and then the next fork trail.

User Story

Every Saturday, Judy finds a running trail to practice jogging. Sometimes when Judy gets to her planned route, she only realizes that the path she chose contains smoke. To that end, she complained to her running partner Amy that she had reached her destination several times and had to go home because of the smell of smoke. The notice details page states that some prescribed fires are scheduled within Pleasanton Ridge Regional Park. Trail Angel detects air pollution in Moller Park. Depending on current wind speed and direction, The Preserve Trail may be affected.
Judy resets her plan to the Calaveras Ridge Regional Trail via the reset trail feature on the notification details page on Trail Angel. Judy never received another AQI notification or alert from Trail Angel before the weekend, so she finished her running training on Saturday and enjoyed her time in nature.

Takeaways

What I Learned

  • Right Segmentation: Accurately defining and targeting the appropriate market segments is crucial for the success of any project. A well-executed segmentation strategy can lead to more effective marketing and product development.
  • Opportunity from Competitors: Analyzing competitors can reveal valuable opportunities for improvement and innovation. Identifying gaps in the market or areas where competitors fall short can guide strategic decision-making.
  • Understanding User Behavior and Biology: A deep understanding of user behavior, including the psychological and biological factors that influence it, is essential for designing products and experiences that resonate with your target audience.
  • Methodology Application to My Project: Ensuring that the chosen methodology is applicable and tailored to your specific project is key to achieving consistent and meaningful results.
  • Brand Strategy Development: Crafting a well-defined brand strategy is vital for establishing a strong brand identity and effectively communicating your brand's values and unique selling points.
  • Utilizing Brand Identity in UX/UI Design: Integrating your brand identity into user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) design helps create a cohesive and memorable user journey, enhancing brand recognition and user engagement.
  • Realistic Tech Implementation: Implementing technology solutions in a realistic and feasible manner is essential to avoid costly setbacks and ensure the successful execution of your project.
